About the Event

It takes a genius to reduce a complicated thing to a simple one. But it requires sheer brilliance to turn something that’s simple into a complicated one. Are you one those people who like seeing things from a unique perspective? Do you love doing things in a crafty and sophisticated manner?

Round 1: pen and paper / multi step arrangement with proper introduction to each team member and description of the contraption to complete any task and send the video for direct selection.

judging criteria: most no. of steps qualifies for next round.

Round 2:
PROBLEM STATEMENT: 

Make a multi- step arrangement .
A step is defined as an action that results in another action working towards the final aim of the setup. The setup designed could be a collection of bits and pieces, parts of useless machines scrapped together to achieve an innovative and imaginative contraption to complete the job at hand.

GENERAL RULES:

  1. A team can consist of maximum 4 members.
  2. The teams must explain their contraption before the first run in front of judges.And write it up in given sheet.
  3. 4 hous of time will be given to  Each team will be given 3 trials to show their contraption.
  4. contraption should not exceed a time limit of 8 minutes.
  5. No limitation in no. Of steps.
  6. Once initiated no human intervention is allowed.
  7. No hazardous materials should be used  The organisers reserve the right to disqualify the team using unsafe materials.
  8. All the materials required making the setup should be brought by the team. 
  9. The teams are required to remove their arrangement and debris after their two runs.  
  10. The teams must adhere to the spirit of healthy competition.
  11. Participants will be provided with a power supply of 220V/50Hz if needed.
  12. The organizers reserve the right to change any of the rules.
  13. The decision of judges will be final and binding to all. 
   

  SCORING CRITERIA:
     A =each no. of steps =5marks
     B =each no. of energy conversation =10 marks
50 marks reward on completion of primary task

No. of Rounds: 2

No. of members in a team: 4

Judging criteria: maximum points
